# Company Sales Policy

## Effective Date: January 1, 2026

This document defines the sales policies, discount authorization thresholds, deal escalation rules, payment terms, proposal requirements, and revenue recognition guidelines for all sales personnel. Adherence to this policy is mandatory and will be reviewed during annual performance evaluations.

---

## 1. Discount Authorization

All discounts must follow these approval thresholds:

| Discount Range | Approval Required |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Up to 5% | Sales Representative (self-service) |
| 5% - 10% | Sales Manager |
| 10% - 20% | VP of Sales |
| Above 20% | Chief Revenue Officer |

Discounts are calculated on the total contract value before taxes and fees. Volume discounts and promotional discounts follow the same thresholds. Bundled discounts that combine multiple product lines must be evaluated against the aggregate discount percentage, not the individual line item discounts.

Sales representatives are expected to document the business justification for any discount exceeding 5%. This documentation must include the competitive landscape, customer lifetime value assessment, and projected renewal rate. The discount justification form is available in the internal sales operations portal and must be submitted before the discount is applied to the opportunity record.

Discounts offered during promotional campaigns (e.g., end-of-quarter accelerators, annual promotions) are pre-approved by the VP of Sales and do not require individual justification, provided they fall within the published promotional discount range. However, stacking promotional discounts with negotiated discounts is prohibited without CRO approval.

Historical discount data shows that deals closed with discounts between 5-10% have a 23% higher renewal rate than deals closed at full price, suggesting that strategic discounting improves long-term customer retention. Sales managers should consider this data when evaluating discount requests.

## 2. Deal Escalation

Deals meeting any of the following criteria require VP of Sales review before closing:

- Total contract value exceeding $250,000
- Multi-year commitments longer than 24 months
- Custom payment terms deviating from standard net 30
- Deals involving competitors' displacement
- Deals with government entities or regulated industries
- Deals requiring custom SLA terms beyond our standard 99.9% uptime guarantee

The escalation process requires the account executive to submit a deal review request at least 5 business days before the anticipated close date. The VP of Sales will convene a deal review board consisting of the regional sales director, solutions architect, and legal counsel to evaluate the deal terms.

For deals exceeding $1,000,000, the Chief Revenue Officer must also approve the deal terms. These deals require a formal executive sponsor from both the selling and buying organizations. The executive sponsor from our organization must be at the VP level or above.

Emergency escalation procedures are available for time-sensitive competitive situations. In such cases, the VP of Sales may provide verbal approval with written confirmation within 24 hours. Emergency escalations must still include all standard documentation.

## 3. Payment Terms

Standard payment terms are net 30 days from invoice date. Exceptions:

- Government contracts: net 45 days
- Enterprise deals over $500,000: milestone-based payments allowed (25% at signing, 25% at delivery, 50% at go-live)
- Annual subscriptions: payment due at start of each contract year
- Quarterly payment plans: available for deals over $100,000 with Sales Manager approval
- Deferred payment starts: up to 90 days deferral available with VP of Sales approval for strategic accounts

Late payment penalties are 1.5% per month on outstanding balances beyond the agreed payment terms. However, sales representatives should work with the finance team before enforcing penalties on strategic accounts to avoid relationship damage.

Wire transfers and ACH payments are preferred. Credit card payments are accepted for deals under $50,000 but incur a 2.9% processing fee that is passed through to the customer unless otherwise negotiated.

For international deals, payments must be in USD unless specifically approved by the CFO. Currency exchange risk is borne by the customer unless a fixed exchange rate clause is negotiated, which requires treasury department approval.

## 4. Proposal Requirements

All proposals must include:

- Itemized pricing breakdown with unit costs and quantities
- Discount justification (if any discount applied)
- Payment schedule aligned with delivery milestones
- Delivery timeline with specific dates and milestones
- Standard terms and conditions reference
- Customer success plan outlining onboarding and training
- Support tier description and escalation procedures
- Data processing agreement (for deals involving personal data)

Proposals must be generated from the approved proposal template in the CRM system. Custom formatting or externally created proposals require Sales Operations approval before being sent to the customer.

All proposals expire 30 days from the date of issue unless a different validity period is specified. Extensions require Sales Manager approval and a new revision of the proposal document.

Competitive displacement proposals must include a migration plan, risk assessment, and a dedicated technical resource allocation plan. These proposals require review by the Solutions Architecture team before submission.

---

## 5. Revenue Recognition

Revenue is recognized upon delivery of services or activation of licenses, in accordance with ASC 606 guidelines. Pre-payments are recorded as deferred revenue until the performance obligations are satisfied.

For milestone-based contracts, revenue is recognized proportionally as each milestone is completed and accepted by the customer. The project management team must confirm milestone completion before revenue can be recognized.

Subscription revenue is recognized ratably over the subscription period. Multi-year subscriptions with annual price escalators are recognized at the escalated rate for each respective year.

Professional services revenue is recognized as services are delivered, typically on a time-and-materials basis unless a fixed-price arrangement has been agreed upon. Fixed-price engagements use the percentage-of-completion method for revenue recognition.

---

## 6. Territory and Account Assignment

Accounts are assigned based on geographic territory, industry vertical, and company size. Territory conflicts must be escalated to the regional sales director within 48 hours of discovery.

Named accounts are protected for 12 months from the date of assignment. Account reassignment requires VP of Sales approval and a transition plan that ensures continuity of customer relationships.

Channel partner deals follow the same discount and escalation policies but are managed through the partner operations team. Partner-sourced deals receive a 10% commission premium to incentivize partner engagement.

---

## 7. Forecasting and Pipeline Management

All sales representatives must maintain accurate pipeline data in the CRM system. Opportunities must be updated weekly with current stage, close date, and amount.

Forecast categories must align with the following stage definitions:

- Pipeline: early-stage opportunities with identified need
- Best Case: opportunities with confirmed budget and timeline
- Commit: opportunities with verbal agreement or signed proposal
- Closed Won: fully executed contracts

Quarterly forecast accuracy is measured and impacts variable compensation. Representatives with forecast accuracy below 70% for two consecutive quarters will be placed on a performance improvement plan.

---

## 8. Compliance and Ethics

All sales activities must comply with applicable laws and regulations, including anti-bribery laws (FCPA, UK Bribery Act), data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA), and industry-specific regulations.

Gifts and entertainment for customers must not exceed $250 per person per quarter. Any exception requires Chief Compliance Officer approval. Government customers are subject to stricter limits as defined by applicable procurement regulations.

Sales representatives must complete annual compliance training and certify their understanding of these policies. Failure to complete training by the designated deadline will result in suspension of deal approval privileges.

---

This policy applies to all sales personnel globally and must be reviewed annually. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. Questions about policy interpretation should be directed to the Sales Operations team.
